About mirror

Mirror is a clothing store. Their goal is to have affordable clothes for everyone. Mirror would like to go online to keep up with its competitors and customer requests. A responsive e-commerce website would help Mirror leverage online sales opportunities.

Research

I conducted interviews with 5 participants within ages 20-40, to get a better understanding of who I’m designing for. These interviews were conducted either in person or via phone calls.

Goals:

  • What drives people to buy clothes?
  • What factors affect decision making and shopping satisfaction?
  • What do they worry about?

Persona

Based on my research, my persona represents a young, working professional who shops frequently.

Empathy Map

To better understand Maya, I created an empathy map visualizing what Maya would see, hear and think that would influence her shopping experience.

Storyboard

I created a storyboard to illustrate shopping experiences of my persona. These highlight some frustrations she would experience as a shopper, along with her thought process while shopping online.

Usability Testing

User testing with the InVision prototype was conducted with 5 participants in-person and remotely. All users were able to easily navigate the website and find the product details page.

Most users found the website clean and clear. Some loved the focus on imagery.

Key findings based on user test results were summarized into an affinity map

Reflections and Next Steps

Through this project, I learned design methods and processes. One of the biggest challenges was to gain insights from the interviews - here, I found empathy maps useful.

Nailing information architecture is key for effortless navigation. Revisioning the sitemap based on card sorts and task scenarios proved extremely useful. When in doubt, test!

The next steps are to replicate and adapt the templates and styles created to all the remaining pages of Mirror including the checkout and payment process along with the about and help pages. As mirror curates it's products gets a fresh set of clothes every month, working on the newsletter and updating shoppers about trending clothes would be very interesting
